# Beef Wellington (Julia Child–Style)
Title: Beef Wellington
Source: 
Yield: 6-8 servings
Prep Time: 
Cook Time: 
Total Time: 
Cuisine: British/French
Category: Dinner

## Description
Classic Beef Wellington prepared using Julia Child’s method: seared beef tenderloin, mushroom duxelles, prosciutto, and puff pastry. Designed for holiday service with proper resting and structure.

## Ingredients

### Beef & Wrap
- 2.5–3 lb beef tenderloin (center cut)
- 8–10 slices prosciutto
- 1 sheets puff pastry
- 3 tbsp Dijon mustard
- 1 tsp thyme, chopped
- 1 egg, beaten (for egg wash)
- Kosher salt
- Black pepper

### Duxelles
- 2 lb mushrooms (button or cremini)
- 1/4 cup dry vermouth or sherry
- 2 shallots, minced
- 2 tbsp unsalted butter
- 1 tbsp fresh thyme, minced
- Salt and pepper

### Sauce
- 2 tbsp butter
- 1 shallot, minced
- 1/2 cup sherry
- 1 cup beef broth (chicken will do)
- 1 cup heavy cream
- 2 tsp coarse ground black pepper
- 1 tbsp Dijon Mustard
- 4 sprigs fresh thyme

## Instructions

1. **Prepare the Beef**
   - Trim and tie the tenderloin.
   - Season generously with salt and pepper.
   - Sear on all sides in a hot pan until browned.
   - Remove, cool completely, and brush with Dijon mustard.

2. **Prepare the Duxelles**
   - Finely chop mushrooms.
   - Cook with butter, shallots, thyme, salt, and pepper until completely dry.
   - Cool fully.

3. **Assemble**
   - Lay prosciutto on plastic wrap, overlapping slightly.
   - Spread duxelles evenly over prosciutto.
   - Place beef on top and wrap tightly.
   - Chill 20–30 minutes.

4. **Wrap in Pastry**
   - Roll puff pastry.
   - Wrap beef tightly; seal seams.
   - Brush with egg wash.
   - Chill again 15–20 minutes.

5. **Bake**
   - Bake at 425°F (220°C) for 40–45 minutes.
   - Target internal temperature: 125–130°F.
   - Rest 20–30 minutes before slicing.

6. **Sauce**
   - Heat butter in a skillet
   - Cook shallots until translucent
   - deglaze with the sherry
   - add in the cream and herbs, and simmer slowly
   - serve over wellington

## Notes
- Resting is mandatory to retain juices.
- Can be assembled one day ahead and baked day-of.

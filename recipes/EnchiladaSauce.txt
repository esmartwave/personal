Title: Homemade Red Enchilada Sauce
Source: https://cookieandkate.com/enchilada-sauce-recipe/
Yield: ~2 cups (16 oz)
Prep Time: 3 minutes
Cook Time: 7 minutes
Total Time: 10 minutes
Cuisine: Mexican
Category: Sauce / Condiment

Ingredients:
- 3 tablespoons olive oil
- 3 tablespoons flour (whole wheat flour, all-purpose flour, or gluten-free flour blend)
- 1 tablespoon ground chili powder
- 1 teaspoon ground cumin
- ½ teaspoon garlic powder
- ¼ teaspoon dried oregano
- ¼ teaspoon salt (or to taste)
- Pinch of cinnamon (optional but recommended)
- 2 tablespoons tomato paste
- 2 cups vegetable broth
- 1 teaspoon apple cider vinegar or distilled white vinegar
- Freshly ground black pepper (to taste)

Instructions:
1. Measure and arrange all dry ingredients near the stove: flour, chili powder, cumin, garlic powder, oregano, salt, and optional cinnamon. Keep the tomato paste and broth nearby as well. :contentReference[oaicite:1]{index=1}
2. In a medium pot over medium heat, warm the olive oil until a light sprinkle of the flour/spice mixture sizzles on contact (this may take a couple of minutes). :contentReference[oaicite:2]{index=2}
3. Add the flour and spice mixture to the hot oil. Whisk constantly and cook until fragrant and slightly deeper in color, about 1 minute. :contentReference[oaicite:3]{index=3}
4. Whisk in the tomato paste, then slowly pour in the vegetable broth while whisking to prevent lumps. :contentReference[oaicite:4]{index=4}
5. Raise heat to medium-high and bring the sauce to a simmer. Reduce heat to maintain a gentle simmer and cook, whisking often, about 5–7 minutes until the sauce thickens slightly. :contentReference[oaicite:5]{index=5}
6. Remove from heat. Whisk in the vinegar and season with freshly ground black pepper and additional salt if needed. :contentReference[oaicite:6]{index=6}

Notes:
- The sauce thickens further as it cools. :contentReference[oaicite:7]{index=7}
- Store in the refrigerator up to 5 days or freeze up to 3 months. :contentReference[oaicite:8]{index=8}
- To make gluten-free, use a gluten-free flour blend. :contentReference[oaicite:9]{index=9}

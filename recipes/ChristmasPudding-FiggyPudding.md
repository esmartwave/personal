# Traditional Christmas Pudding (Figgy Pudding)

## Yield
1 large pudding (10–12 servings)

## Description
A classic steamed Christmas pudding made with dried fruits, citrus, and warm spices. Intended to be prepared days or weeks in advance.

## Ingredients

### Dried Fruit
- 2 1 /2 ounces pitted dried dates (about 9)
- 1 1/2 ounces candied lemon peel (or just zest)
- 1 1/2 ounces candied orange peel (or just zest)
- 3/4 cup golden raisins (4 ounces)
- 3/4 cup raisins (4 ounces)
- 2/3 cup zante currants (3 ounces)
- 1/2 cup brandy
- 3 slices white sandwich bread

### Pudding
- 3 tablespoons unsalted butter
- 3/4 cup packed dark brown sugar
- 1/4 cup honey
- 1/2 cup all-purpose flour
- 1 teaspoon ground cinnamon
- 1/2 teaspoon ground allspice
- 1/2 teaspoon baking powder
- 1/2 teaspoon ground cloves
- 1/2 teaspoon ground ginger
- 1/2 teaspoon ground nutmeg
- 1/2 teaspoon kosher salt
- 1 medium sweet apple, such as Fuji or Honeycrisp
- juice from 1 medium lemon
- juice from 1 small orange
- 2 large eggs
- 1 tablespoon black treacle or molasses
- 4 ounces shredded beef suet (about 1 cup), or 4 tablespoons frozen unsalted butter plus 1/4 cup frozen  vegetable shortening (I also used Coconut oil)

### Serve With (any of these)
- Juice of 1 orange
- Juice of 1 lemon
- Powdered sugar
- 3 tablespoons brandy
- Heavy cream, brandy butter, or hard sauce

## Instructions

1. Combine all dried fruits, citrus zest, and juices in a bowl. Soak overnight.
2. Cream butter and sugar until light.
3. Beat in eggs one at a time.
4. Fold in fruit mixture.
5. Add breadcrumbs, flour, spices, and salt. Mix until combined.
6. Grease a pudding basin. Fill with batter.
7. Cover with parchment and foil, secure tightly.
8. Steam for 4 hours, checking water level regularly.
9. Cool completely. Store wrapped in refrigerator.
10. Re-steam 1–2 hours before serving.

## Notes
- Improves with aging.
- Serve with cream, custard, or brandy sauce.
